The Remlo architecture bridges traditional banking and on-chain payroll execution on the Tempo L1 network. Here is a high-level view of how funds move from an employer's bank account to an employee's local bank.

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[Employer] -->|USD| B(Bridge Virtual Account)
    B -->|Mint pathUSD| C(PayrollTreasury)
    C -->|Execute Run| D(PayrollBatcher)
    D -->|Distribute| E(Employee Wallets)
    E -->|Spend/Off-ramp| F(Bridge Visa Cards)
    F -->|Local Currency| G([Local Banks])
```

## Employer Funding
The employer initiates the flow by depositing funds into a Bridge virtual account. Bridge automatically handles the conversion from traditional currency to stablecoins (pathUSD) and mints these tokens directly into the employer's account within the PayrollTreasury contract on Tempo L1.

## PayrollTreasury
The PayrollTreasury is the secure on-chain vault where employer funds reside. It holds the pathUSD and tracks the available balance and gas budget for each employer. All payroll runs originate from this treasury.

## PayrollBatcher
When a payroll run is executed—either manually via the dashboard or autonomously via the MPP API—the PayrollBatcher coordinates the simultaneous payout to all employees. It pulls the necessary funds from the treasury and distributes them, attaching the appropriate TIP-20 memo to each transfer for compliance and tracking. 

## Employee Wallets & Bridge
Employees receive their salary directly into their on-chain wallets on Tempo. From there, they can use their connected Bridge Visa cards to spend the funds in the real world or seamlessly off-ramp the balance to their local bank accounts.
