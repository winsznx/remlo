# Remlo

Remlo is a payroll operating system for globally distributed teams that want one place to fund treasury, onboard workers, settle payroll on-chain, manage spend controls, and expose the same workflows to software agents.

It blends five layers into one product:

| Layer | What it gives you |
| --- | --- |
| Employer workspace | Team onboarding, payroll runs, treasury visibility, compliance controls, cards, and billing |
| Employee portal | Balance, payment history, wallet identity, cards, and bank off-ramp |
| Tempo contracts | Treasury accounting, payroll settlement, salary streaming, employee registry, and yield routing |
| Bridge and Privy | Fiat rails, cards, KYC, identity, and wallet creation |
| Machine-paid APIs | HTTP 402 and session-based access to payroll and treasury workflows |

## What teams use Remlo for

### Employer operations

- Fund one on-chain payroll treasury instead of managing one payout at a time
- Invite employees without forcing them to handle raw wallet setup
- Run compliant batch payroll with memo-backed audit context
- Track treasury, locked payroll, and yield from one operator console

### Employee experience

- Accept an invite and claim a wallet in minutes
- See every payment with status, memo context, and explorer proof
- Spend with a card or off-ramp to bank rails where Bridge is enabled

### Machine access

- Price treasury and payroll workflows for agents and internal automation
- Open sessions for repeated reads instead of paying per request
- Reuse the same product data model across UI, APIs, and assistants

## How Remlo works

1. An employer signs in, creates a workspace, and syncs a canonical payroll wallet.
2. Employees are invited, claimed, and linked to wallets, KYC state, and payout settings.
3. Treasury is funded on Tempo and tracked through `PayrollTreasury`.
4. Payroll preparation builds memos, runs policy checks, and creates a draft run.
5. Settlement executes through `PayrollBatcher`, while yield and streaming salary remain readable from the same employer identity.
6. Employees see payouts in `/portal`, and agents can access the same surface through paid endpoints.

## The three concepts worth knowing first

- Remlo currently runs on Tempo test infrastructure, so treasury, payroll, and card flows should be treated as sandbox workflows unless your deployment says otherwise.
- Treasury, yield, and payroll contract state resolve from the canonical employer wallet, not from an off-chain company id.
- Invite links are onboarding paths, not permanent access URLs. Once an employee record is claimed, the original invite should no longer be reused.
